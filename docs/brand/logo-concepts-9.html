Identity system · v1

Pivot / the Product Map mark

The chosen direction, made real. Pivot is a plan-sheet folded at a crease — depth, phases, and the idiom of "turning the corner toward what's next." This sheet locks the mark's geometry, the wordmark and lockups, color, motion, the rules, and ships the exported files. The signature device is the two-face fold: a shadowed front and a lit, receding back — the same light/shadow logic carries into the wordmark and the whole system.

01 — The mark · primary + mono
__PRIMARY__
Pivot · primary (two-tone fold)
__MONO__

Refinement from v8. The face-off mark was a flat, symmetric parallelogram. The canonical Pivot adds perspective — the back face foreshortens and its top/bottom edges converge — so the fold reads as genuine depth, not two stuck rectangles. Below: the path we took.

04 — Wordmark & lockups

The wordmark echoes the fold. Set in Sora 800, tight tracking (−0.03em). “Product” takes the shadow tone, “Map” the lit cobalt — the two colours are the two faces of the mark. No decoration; the type stays quiet so the symbol leads.
